About Us

Rooted in tradition. Open to all who honor the earth.

Our Mission

The Earthcentered Traditions Collective exists to foster a welcoming, inclusive community for individuals who follow or are curious about earth-centered spiritual traditions. We celebrate the cycles of nature, honor ancestral wisdom, and support each member's personal spiritual journey.

We believe that when people come together to mark the turning of the seasons, share knowledge across traditions, and support one another, something profound and healing takes place. Our collective is that place.

Our Story

The Earthcentered Traditions Collective began in 2019 as a small study circle of five people who wanted a local, non-dogmatic space to explore nature spirituality together. Meeting in living rooms and at local parks, the group grew steadily through word of mouth.

By 2022, the collective had grown to over forty regular participants representing a wide range of paths — Wiccan, Druidic, Heathen, animist, eclectic, and tradition-curious. We established a formal structure, adopted a code of conduct, and began hosting larger seasonal celebrations open to the wider community.

Today we continue to grow, welcoming new members while staying true to our core values of respect, curiosity, and deep reverence for the natural world.

Traditions We Honor

Our collective is non-dogmatic and tradition-inclusive. Members bring perspectives from a wide range of earth-centered paths:

  • Wicca & Witchcraft — both traditional and eclectic
  • Druidry — Celtic nature spirituality and philosophy
  • Heathenry & Ásatrú — Norse and Germanic traditions
  • Animism — the belief in the spirit of all living things
  • Folk Magic & Herbalism — regional and cultural plant traditions
  • Eclectic Paganism — personal spiritual paths drawing from many sources
  • Nature Spirituality — non-theistic earth reverence

If you walk a different earth-centered path not listed here, you are equally welcome. We celebrate diversity of tradition as one of our greatest strengths.
